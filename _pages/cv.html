Education

PhD in Computer Science, University of Milano-Bicocca

Start: 2019 - End: 2023

The main subject of my PhD regards machine learning under uncertainty, focusing on weakly supervised learning, cautious prediction and uncertainty management, both from an algorithmic/theoretical as well as experimental point of view. During my PhD I also collaborated with different hospitals, in Italy (IRCCS Istituto Ortopedico Galeazzi, IRCCS Ospedale San Raffaele) and abroad, on medical machine learning projects, also applying the above mentioned techniques. My supervisors were Davide Ciucci and Federico Cabitza.

Visiting Student, Heudiasyc, Université de Compiègne

Start: Sep 2021 - End: Dec 2021

During my PhD I spent 3 months as a visiting student at the Heudiasyc research lab, under the supervision of prof. Thierry Denoeux. During this period, my main research projects regarded studying the relationships between two important uncertainty representation theories (rough set theory and belief functions) as well as their applications in knowledge representation and machine learning, especially in clustering.

Training Course in "Antropo-Psycho-Pedagogical Disciplines

Start: Jan 2019 - End: Apr 2019

I graduated from a state-recognized program to train teacher and educators, focusing on the following subjects: intercultural education, educational planning and evaluation methodologies, special education.

Master of Science in Computer Science, University of Milano-Bicocca

Final Vote: 110 cum laude
Start: 2015 - End: 2017

The subject of my thesis regarded the study of an algebraic model of uncertainty (orthopairs) and its relationships with other uncertainty models. In particular, I was interested in studying the generalization of information theory to this setting, as well as the applications of these ideas in the setting of clustering.
My studies were mostly focused on theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence. The main courses I followed were: machine learning, probabilistic graphical models, representation of uncertainty, theoretical computer science and computational complexity, non-classical logics, information theory, high-performance computing, dynamical systems theory

Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, University of Milano-Bicocca

Final Vote: 110 cum laude
Start: 2012 - End: 2015

The subject of my thesis regarded the use of causal graphical models for inferring and representing the evolution of cancer. In particular, I designed a pipeline for cancer data analysis based on these models.
My studies were mostly focused on theoretical computer science and data sciences. The main courses I followed were: algorithms and data structures, bioinformatics, probability and statistics, operations research and optimization, models of concurrent and dynamical systems, databases, mathematics (analysis, linear algebra, logics), computability and language theory, programming (Java, C/C++, Prolog, Lisp).

Work and Teaching Experience

Guest Lecturer on Weakly Supervised Feature Selection and Uncertainty in Dynamical Systems, MSc in Computer Science, University of Milano-Bicocca

Start: June 2021 - End: June 2021

I teached two 2hrs lessons within the Master-level course "Complex and Uncertain Systems". The first lesson regarded the application of rough set theory and belief functions to feature selection in weakly supervised learning problems.
