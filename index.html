About me

I'm a Postdoc Researcher at the IRCCS Ospedale Galeazzi-Sant'Ambrogio, the major Italian research hospital devoted to orthopaedics and traumatology, where I work on applications of machine learning methodologies and human-AI interaction to clinical decision making and clinical process management. I also work as a Lecturer at the University of Milano-Bicocca and I'm a member of the MUDI (Modeling Uncertainty, Decisions and Interaction) lab at the same university. Previously I obtained my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Milano-Bicocca, working in the MUDI lab.

My main research interests are uncertainty management, machine learning, medical AI and human-AI interaction, as well as their intersection. Therefore, I'm primarily interested in (and authored publications related to):
  • Weakly supervised learning
  • Cautious inference, uncertainty quantification and conformal prediction
  • Soft clustering
  • Fair, Accountable and Transparent ML
  • Applications of ML and uncertainty modeling in medical decision-making
  • Human-AI interaction and user-centered AI
My work has appeared in the top-ranked venues focusing on AI, uncertainty representation and human-computer interaction (including AAAI, CHI, ECAI, MDAI, FUZZ-IEEE, INTERACT, VISIGRAPP as well as several Q1 journals) as well as in health-oriented or multi-disciplinary venues (Artifical Intelligence in Medicine, International Journal of Medical Informatics, Clinical Chemistry and Laboratory Medicine). I'm also interested in, and have authored publications on, the following topics:
  • Formalisms for representing uncertainty (rough sets, fuzzy sets, belief functions, imprecise probabilities)
  • Theoretical computer science (esp. learning theory/theoretical ML, dynamical systems, logics, algorithm design)
  • Robustness, data reliability and robust ML
  • Socio-technical and philosophical issues related to computer science and AI
